My thoughts on Certifications

I mostly have AWS Certifications so I can provide my thoughts on these certifications and the impacts pursuing them has had on my life and career.

Positives


For me personally , obtaining certifications opened a lot of doors and provided opportunities within and outside the company I was working for at the time. It also helped with networking as it develops a sense of community around individuals who have particular certifications and others who wish to pursue them. I have developed relationships with a lot of people through discussions about certifications and the ecosystem surrounded by it such as prep material , official documentation , and such.

Obtaining certifications helps cover a broad range of material and introduces people to technology and their use cases. From my experience with AWS certifications - I have a solid grasp of what most of the services do and how they interact with each other. This has proven to be a valuable skill in teams and individual projects. It also proves as a good foundation for products that have to be developed using the respective technology. The AWS ecosystem also introduced me to several best practices in software engineering such as disaster recovery , fault tolerance , scalable architecture, security, and several other concepts that carry over into my skillset as a software engineer.

Negatives


Certifications provide an abstracted, high-level view of the products and solutions. Having a certification on it's own does not provide too much inherent value in terms of being a better engineer or proving competence in the respective domain. My opinion is that an individual with a SysOps Administrator certificate from AWS but someone who has no experience as a SysOps Administrator will definitely have to spend time learning the fundamentals and developing their skillset in order to become a valuable , effective SysOps Administrator.

Concluding Thoughts


At the end of the day, certifications have to be combined with personal projects and relevant work experience and practice to be truly effective. Certifications are extremely valuable for opening the door to new opportunities and networks and can set individuals apart in their respective domain.
